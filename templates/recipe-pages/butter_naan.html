Butter Naan

This Butter Naan, or for that matter any other naan and rich North Indian gravies always make a great pair. So, on days when I don’t make the usual chapati or roti at home, I end up making naans. These are easy to make, light and soft Indian flatbreads. These flatbreads are always made with all-purpose flour. But the same soft texture can be achieved with whole wheat flour (atta) too. I have used the regular chapati atta for making this recipe, but you can opt to make them with only all-purpose flour. Other ingredients that go in this recipe are sugar, instant yeast and curd (yogurt). Generally, Butter Naan that is served in restaurants is only slathered with butter after it gets cooked. However, I always add some butter in the dough and then spread some butter on the top as well, while serving. As I mentioned earlier, this Butter Naan is essentially cooked in a tandoor or clay oven in restaurants. But at home, you can cook them in an oven or on a tawa or skillet.

ingredients

1. 3 cups whole wheat flour ((atta))

2. 1 tablespoon sugar

3. 1 cup water (or as required)

4. 3 to 4 tablespoon Curd ((yogurt))

5. 2 tablespoons Butter (- softened, unsalted or salted)

6. ¾ teaspoon salt (or as required)

7. Butter (- softened or melted, as required, for brushing on the naan, unsalted or salted)
